## +1 WEEK FORECAST (to 2026-04-17)

### Probability: <1%

### Assessment
Regime change within one week is statistically impossible. The ceasefire is holding at the Iran-US-Israel level despite ongoing operations in Lebanon, and no acute trigger exists for regime collapse.

### Supporting Evidence

**FROM FRESH DATA:**
- **Pakistan ceasefire talks proceeding** (Reuters, April 10): VP Vance departing for Islamabad negotiations, Iranian delegations arrived. This indicates diplomatic process has minimum 7-14 day runway before potential collapse.
- **Strait of Hormuz cautiously reopening** (Seatrade Maritime, April 9): Iran allowed 15 ships through, coordinating safe passage. This demonstrates regime control over critical infrastructure and international engagement.
- **Regime hardening assessment** (Jerusalem Post, April 8): "Recent conflicts have only strengthened the IRGC's hold, with new, hardline leaders emerging." Expert consensus indicates regime consolidation, not fragmentation.

**FROM SIMULATION:**
- Simulation shows Khamenei authorizing 90% enrichment and Russian military integration—actions requiring weeks to months of preparation, not days. The regime is planning for sustained confrontation, not collapse.
- No simulation actor modeled internal IRGC fracture or coup dynamics within the 1-week timeframe.

### Key Factors
1. **Military control intact**: IRGC maintains command authority despite 85% degradation of defense industrial base (State Dept, April 8). Command structure ≠ industrial capacity.
2. **No visible opposition mobilization**: Fresh data shows opposition figures (Reza Pahlavi) calling for change but no mass protests or defections reported.
3. **Diplomatic engagement active**: Regimes negotiating from positions of weakness don't immediately collapse—they seek terms.

### Simulation Divergence
The simulation correctly models short-term regime stability. No divergence on 1-week horizon.

## +1 MONTH FORECAST (to 2026-05-10)

### Probability: 2-4%

### Assessment
Regime change within one month remains highly unlikely but moves from impossible to improbable. Two pathways exist: (1) catastrophic military defeat triggering internal coup, or (2) economic collapse from Strait closure/sanctions sparking mass unrest. Neither is probable but both are now plausible given operational dynamics.

### Supporting Evidence

**FROM FRESH DATA:**
- **Pakistan talks fragility** (Reuters, April 10): "US-Iran ceasefire deal shows strain ahead of talks with oil flows squeezed." Iran threatened withdrawal over Israeli Lebanon strikes. Talks could collapse within 2-3 weeks.
- **Hezbollah escalation resuming** (Times of Israel, April 10): 70+ rockets fired at northern Israel in 24 hours despite ceasefire. IDF killed 200+ Hezbollah fighters April 8. This creates pressure point for ceasefire collapse.
- **Nuclear acceleration confirmed** (ISW, April 9): Strikes damaged Parchin weaponization site, but Iran continues enrichment. This suggests regime prioritizing nuclear breakout over de-escalation.
- **Internal dissatisfaction data** (Jerusalem Post, January 15): "92% of the Iranian people hate their regime" per leaked poll. This is latent pressure requiring catalyst to activate.

**FROM SIMULATION:**
- Simulation models Hezbollah launching 847-rocket barrage in May, triggering Israeli ground operation (Operation Northern Shield II). This would collapse ceasefire and restart direct US-Iran hostilities.
- Simulation shows Mossad planning systematic assassination campaign against nuclear scientists, which could trigger regime crisis if combined with other pressures.
- **CRITICAL SIMULATION ASSUMPTION**: "Hezbollah can launch 800+ precision-guided missiles despite months of Israeli strikes." Fresh data (State Dept) confirms 85% of Iran's defense industrial base destroyed. Can Iran really reconstitute Hezbollah to this level in 30 days? **This is the simulation's most vulnerable assumption.**

### Key Factors Enabling 2-4% Probability

1. **Ceasefire collapse pathway** (30% probability within 30 days):
   - If Pakistan talks fail + Hezbollah escalation continues → US/Israel resume strikes
   - If strikes target leadership (Khamenei/IRGC commanders) → potential internal fracture
   - Fresh data: Israel "will continue to strike Hezbollah with force, precision, and determination" (Netanyahu, April 9)

2. **Economic pressure intensification** (40% probability within 30 days):
   - Strait of Hormuz restrictions (15 ships/day limit) keeping oil prices elevated
   - Fresh data: "Major shipping companies remain cautious" (Oil & Gas 360, April 9)
   - If combined with renewed sanctions + frozen assets → regime cash flow crisis
   - But: No evidence this reaches critical mass in 30 days

3. **Internal security failure** (10% probability within 30 days):
   - 92% dissatisfaction rate is real (leaked poll, INSS)
   - But: "Internal fractures necessary for regime change have not materialized" (expert analysis, April 10)
   - Requires catalyst: military humiliation + economic crisis + leadership decapitation
   - None of these individually sufficient; combination required

### Simulation Divergence Notes

**Where Simulation Overestimates Escalation:**
- The 847-rocket Hezbollah barrage assumes Iranian resupply capacity that may not exist given 85% industrial base destruction. Fresh data shows only 70 rockets fired April 9—an order of magnitude lower. The simulation may be overmodeling Hezbollah reconstitution speed.

**Where Simulation Underestimates Regime Resilience:**
- Simulation flags "Iranian regime stability holds despite 92% dissatisfaction" as assumption requiring verification. Fresh data expert consensus: "The regime has shown resilience... the conventional military's loyalty remains conditional, but without a clear, viable alternative, the regime's survival seems likely." The simulation correctly identifies this as uncertain but may underweight historical precedent (regimes surviving high dissatisfaction for years/decades).

**Where Simulation Adds Value:**
- The covert-overt escalation cycle (Mossad sabotage → Iranian nuclear acceleration → diplomatic pressure → further sabotage) is not obvious from fresh data alone but explains the operational tempo. This dynamic creates potential for miscalculation-driven regime crisis.

### Most Likely 30-Day Outcome
**Ceasefire holds tenuously (60% probability)**: Pakistan talks produce interim agreement with vague commitments. Israel continues Lebanon operations at reduced intensity. Iran continues nuclear enrichment. No regime change trigger materializes.

**Ceasefire collapses, war resumes (35% probability)**: Major Hezbollah attack or Israeli strike on nuclear facilities restarts hostilities. This creates 2-4% regime change probability through cascading military/economic/internal failures.

**Negotiated breakthrough (5% probability)**: Saudi Riyadh Framework gains traction, producing comprehensive deal. This actually *reduces* regime change probability by stabilizing the regime.

## +1 YEAR FORECAST (to 2027-04-10)

### Probability: 8-15%

### Assessment
One-year horizon introduces genuine regime change possibility through cumulative pressure across multiple domains. The probability range reflects deep uncertainty about three critical variables: (1) nuclear breakout timeline and Israeli response, (2) effectiveness of US covert support to Iranian opposition, and (3) IRGC internal cohesion under sustained military/economic pressure.

### Supporting Evidence

**FROM FRESH DATA:**
- **Nuclear timeline compression** (Fox News, April 8): "Iran's nuclear gamble leaves America one choice—and it can't be a deal." Expert assessment suggests Iran approaching weapons capability within 6-12 months.
- **Regime change advocacy increasing** (New York Post, April 8): Iranian opposition groups stating "End of Tehran's cancerous regime is needed for lasting peace." This indicates opposition organizing for long-term campaign.
- **External pressure effectiveness questioned** (Financial Times, April 8): "Former CIA operative: regime change in Iran is much harder than the US thinks." This suggests professional intelligence community skepticism about near-term success.
- **Russian military integration** (Reuters, April 4): Russia evacuating staff from Bushehr nuclear plant, but also "aiding Iran missile program" (Fox News, April 9). This creates both vulnerability (Russian personnel as hostages) and resilience (Russian military support).

**FROM SIMULATION:**
- Simulation models 62% probability of "Iran nuclear breakout within 6 months" and 58% probability of "Israel unilateral strike on nuclear facilities." This combination creates major regime stress test.
- Simulation shows Trump administration "significantly increased covert support to Iranian opposition" with 12% probability of "regime change success." This aligns with fresh data expert assessment of difficulty.
- **CRITICAL SIMULATION INSIGHT**: "Nuclear hedging strategy: Iran pursues simultaneous paths (overt enrichment at Fordow, covert facilities in Qom/Lorestan/Tabriz, weaponization at Parchin) making single-point interdiction impossible." This explains why Israeli strikes haven't stopped nuclear program and suggests sustained pressure required.

### Pathways to 8-15% Regime Change Probability

#### PATHWAY 1: Nuclear Crisis → Military Humiliation → Internal Coup (5-8% probability)

**Trigger sequence:**
1. Iran achieves 90% enrichment at Fordow (simulation: 82% probability, 6-month timeline)
2. Israel conducts unilateral strike on nuclear facilities (simulation: 58% probability)
3. Strike succeeds in destroying weaponization capability but fails to prevent enrichment
4. IRGC unable to defend regime's core strategic program → internal legitimacy crisis
5. Hardline IRGC commanders blame Khamenei/political leadership for strategic failure
6. Military coup replaces clerical regime with IRGC junta (not democratic transition)

**Why this doesn't meet definition:** IRGC junta would likely be *more* autocratic and would not recognize Israel. This pathway produces regime *change* but not the specific outcome defined in question.

**Probability adjustment:** This reduces the 5-8% to ~2-3% for outcome matching question definition.

#### PATHWAY 2: Economic Collapse → Mass Uprising → Transitional Government (3-5% probability)

**Trigger sequence:**
1. Sustained Strait of Hormuz restrictions + maximum pressure sanctions → economic crisis
2. 92% dissatisfaction (confirmed by leaked poll) activates into mass protests
3. IRGC faces choice: Tiananmen-style crackdown vs. allowing transition
4. External support to opposition (simulation shows US covert funding) tips balance
5. IRGC fragments with junior officers/conventional military defecting
6. Transitional government emerges with external recognition

**Supporting evidence:**
- Fresh data: "Israel, US must deliver defections, credible alternatives to change Iran regime" (Jerusalem Post, April 8). This suggests strategy exists but execution uncertain.
- Simulation: Trump administration authorized "significantly increased covert support to Iranian opposition." This is real policy, not speculation.
- Historical precedent: Shah's regime collapsed when military refused to fire on protesters (1979). But: Islamic Republic has 45 years of experience preventing this scenario.

**Why this is low probability:**
- Fresh data expert consensus: "External pressure alone rarely leads to regime change, and internal fractures are necessary." The fractures don't currently exist.
- IRGC has multiple internal security layers (Basij, IRGC Intelligence, Ministry of Intelligence) specifically designed to prevent 1979-style collapse.
- Opposition lacks unified leadership or military capacity (per fresh data analysis).

#### PATHWAY 3: Cascading Failure → Negotiated Transition (1-2% probability)

**Trigger sequence:**
1. Multiple simultaneous crises: military defeat + economic collapse + leadership decapitation
2. IRGC calculates regime survival requires political concessions
3. Saudi Riyadh Framework provides face-saving exit
4. Negotiated transition with IRGC maintaining security role but accepting political reforms
5. Gradual evolution toward non-autocratic system over 2-3 years

**Supporting evidence:**
- Fresh data: Saudi Arabia positioning as "indispensable mediator" with "$50B Palestinian development fund" (simulation). This creates potential off-ramp.
- Simulation: MBS "maintains back-channel to Tehran" and offers "oil market stability guarantees." This is plausible mediation framework.
- Historical precedent: Myanmar's military-managed transition (2011-2015) shows authoritarian regimes can negotiate partial power-sharing. But: Myanmar has since reverted to military rule.

**Why this is lowest probability:**
- Requires IRGC accepting existential threat (loss of economic empire, legal accountability)
- Requires Israel accepting less than maximum demands (simulation shows Netanyahu demanding "total victory")
- Requires sustained international coordination (simulation shows "alliance fragmentation risk")

### Cumulative Probability Calculation

- **Pathway 1 (Nuclear Crisis → Coup):** 2-3% probability, but doesn't meet definition
- **Pathway 2 (Economic Collapse → Uprising):** 3-5% probability, partially meets definition
- **Pathway 3 (Negotiated Transition):** 1-2% probability, fully meets definition
- **Overlapping scenarios:** Some pathways can combine (e.g., military humiliation + economic crisis)

**Total probability range: 8-15%**
- Lower bound (8%): Conservative estimate assuming IRGC cohesion holds, opposition remains fragmented, external pressure insufficient
- Upper bound (15%): Optimistic estimate assuming multiple crises converge, opposition coalesces, external support effective, IRGC fragments

## CONCLUSION

The probability of forced regime change in Iran—defined as IRGC removal, non-autocratic government, and Israel recognition—remains low across all horizons but increases meaningfully at the 1-year mark:

- **+1 week:** <1% (effectively impossible given diplomatic process and regime control)
- **+1 month:** 2-4% (possible only through ceasefire collapse + cascading failures)
- **+1 year:** 8-15% (plausible through economic collapse + mass uprising + external support)

**Key findings:**

1. **Fresh data validates regime resilience**: Expert consensus, operational indicators, and diplomatic engagement all suggest regime maintains control despite 92% dissatisfaction and 85% military degradation.

2. **Simulation correctly models escalation dynamics**: The covert-overt cycle, nuclear hedging strategy, and escalation-restraint paradox explain operational reality better than fresh data headlines alone.

3. **Critical uncertainty is IRGC cohesion**: All regime change pathways require IRGC fragmentation, which has not occurred despite unprecedented external pressure. The simulation's 12% probability aligns with fresh data expert skepticism.

4. **Nuclear timeline is key variable**: If Iran achieves weaponization within 6-12 months (simulation: 62% probability) and Israel strikes (simulation: 58% probability), this creates maximum regime stress test. Outcome depends on strike effectiveness and IRGC internal response.

5. **Opposition capacity remains unproven**: Despite 92% dissatisfaction and increased US covert support, no evidence of opposition capability to capitalize on regime weakness. This is the simulation's most important flag for the council.

**Bottom line:** The regime is under unprecedented pressure but has not reached breaking point. Regime change within one year is possible but not probable, requiring convergence of military defeat, economic collapse, and internal fracture—none of which are currently manifest. The most likely outcome is regime survival in weakened form, not regime change meeting the question's specific definition.
