Israeli operations in Lebanon expand incrementally: eight additional precision strikes over the week target Hezbollah infrastructure within five kilometers of the border, destroying rocket launchers concealed in civilian areas. The strikes are surgical, but Lebanese civilian casualties—fourteen dead, including children in a village near a weapons depot—fuel international condemnation and complicate Israel's diplomatic position.

CENTCOM maintains its carrier strike group in the Gulf of Oman, sustaining enhanced air defense at al-Asad and al-Tanf while ISR assets track IRGC naval activity. The Strait of Hormuz remains open, but ship-tracking data shows Iranian-linked vessels dominating traffic—a quiet assertion of Tehran's geographic leverage. Fifth Fleet commanders note increased IRGC fast-boat activity near Qeshm Island, probing patterns that suggest contingency planning for closure operations.

In Tel Aviv and Haifa, permitted protests draw hundreds chanting against the government's handling of the northern front. In Tehran, labor strikes erupt in three provinces as CIA-supported networks exploit economic grievances and the regime's wartime resource drain. IRGC intelligence arrests forty-seven suspected "foreign agents," but the protests spread faster than suppression efforts.

The Pakistan talks produce no breakthrough. Iran demands formal US guarantees against nuclear facility strikes; America demands verifiable enrichment caps and proxy de-escalation. The gap remains unbridged, but the ceasefire holds—barely—as each side maneuvers for advantage in the next phase.

# WORLD STATE — Turn 2 (May-June 2026)

The ceasefire shatters in blood and fire on May 3rd, when Hezbollah unleashes the largest rocket barrage since 2006. IDF Northern Command headquarters in Safed takes seventeen direct hits; the Rafael facility near Haifa sustains catastrophic damage to its missile production lines; Ben Gurion Airport closes for six hours as Iron Dome batteries expend themselves intercepting salvos aimed at runways. Hezbollah's advance warnings—cryptic social media posts and SMS blasts ninety minutes before launch—allow partial civilian evacuations, but the scale overwhelms Israeli defenses. Forty-three Israeli soldiers die in the Safed strikes; eleven civilians perish despite warnings; 127 wounded flood northern hospitals. The Iranian calculus proves devastatingly effective: massive retaliation for sabotage, calibrated just below the threshold that would trigger full American intervention.

Netanyahu's response is immediate and ferocious. Within eight hours, IAF strikes obliterate Hezbollah's Beirut southern suburb headquarters complex, killing senior military commander Ibrahim Aqil and twenty-three others.
