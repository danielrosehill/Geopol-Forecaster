### Private assessment

> The ceasefire between Iran and the US/Israel creates a strategic window that benefits our interests substantially. The conflict diverted American military and political capital from Ukraine, elevated oil prices supporting our budget despite sanctions, and demonstrated Iran's dependence on Russian air defense systems and diplomatic cover. However, the ceasefire is fragile - Israel continues operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon, and US-Pakistan mediation excludes us entirely, limiting our influence over final arrangements. The regime change question is premature but not impossible - if sustained Western pressure combines with internal unrest (the leaked poll showing 92% opposition is concerning for Tehran), the IRGC could face serious challenges. Our priority is ensuring Iran remains a viable strategic partner and arms customer while avoiding overcommitment that weakens our Ukraine position. The exclusion of France from Lebanon talks and Spain from Gaza monitoring shows Western cohesion fracturing - exploitable through information operations. We must signal continued support to Tehran without triggering Israeli strikes on our Syrian bases or personnel.

### Public statement

> Russia welcomes any genuine diplomatic efforts to establish lasting stability in the Middle East. We maintain our principled position that conflicts must be resolved through dialogue and respect for sovereignty, not through external pressure or interference in internal affairs of states. We will continue our constructive role in the region.

### Concrete action

> Accelerate delivery of Su-35 fighter aircraft and S-400 air defense system components to Iran under existing contracts, with Russian military advisors to provide operational training at Iranian bases. Simultaneously, coordinate with Iranian leadership through secure channels to assess their assessment of regime stability and offer enhanced intelligence-sharing on Western-backed opposition networks. Deploy additional electronic warfare systems to Hmeimim and Tartus to monitor Israeli air activity and ensure early warning of any strikes approaching Russian personnel.
