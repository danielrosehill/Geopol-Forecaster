However, Hezbollah remains the immediate operational problem - they have not honored any meaningful ceasefire and continue launching from civilian infrastructure in Lebanon. Our northern communities remain evacuated. The regime change question is irrelevant to our operational planning - we have no capability or intention to pursue that objective. Our focus must be: restore Iron Dome/David's Sling inventories, maintain pressure on Hezbollah without triggering Iranian re-escalation, and prevent any diplomatic outcome that locks in the status quo ante.

> The IDF has successfully degraded significant Iranian military capabilities while maintaining multi-front readiness. We will continue limited, intelligence-driven operations against Hezbollah infrastructure that poses direct threats to Israeli civilians, consistent with our right to self-defense. We expect full implementation of UNSCR 1701 and the withdrawal of Hezbollah forces north of the Litani River.

> Conduct a series of 8-12 precision airstrikes over the next month targeting Hezbollah command nodes, weapons depots, and rocket launch sites in southern Lebanon, specifically focusing on infrastructure within 5km of the border that threatens northern Israeli communities. Strikes will be carefully calibrated to avoid mass civilian casualties and will not target Lebanese state infrastructure. Simultaneously, accelerate coordination with CENTCOM for priority resupply of Iron Dome and David's Sling interceptors to restore stockpiles to 85% of pre-war levels.
