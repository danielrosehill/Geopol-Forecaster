# Reviewer: probabilistic

*Stage B — cross-review round: this lens reviewing all other lenses' answers.*

# PEER REVIEW: Iran-Israel-US Ceasefire Durability Forecasts

## Methodology
I evaluated each response on:
1. **Specificity** - Named actors, concrete actions, calibrated timelines
2. **Fidelity to data** - Integration of fresh data and simulation evidence
3. **Internal consistency** - Logical coherence across time windows
4. **Lens-appropriate sharpness** - Avoiding hedge-speak, making real forecasts

---

## Response A (Neutral Lens) - GRADE: A-

**Strengths:**
- **Exceptional data integration**: Directly quotes fresh data (IDF killed 200+ Hezbollah fighters April 8, Hezbollah paused attacks, Hormuz 10-15 vessels/day) and cross-references simulation predictions
- **Rigorous probability calibration**: 62% (24h), 28% (72h), 15% (1wk), 8% (1mo) with explicit 90% confidence intervals
- **Surgical divergence analysis**: Identifies where simulation overpredicts (Hezbollah retaliation 78% vs observed pause) and underpredicts (Israeli strike intensity)
- **Falsifiable triggers**: "Confirmed Hezbollah rocket barrage >50 rockets" would shift 24h probability to 25-35%

**Weaknesses:**
- **Overconfidence in 24h window**: 62% seems high given Hezbollah already resumed attacks (70 rockets April 9 per fresh data). The "decision-making lag" rationale doesn't account for this.
- **Underweights Netanyahu trial pressure**: Fresh data shows trial resumes April 13, but this isn't incorporated as escalation driver until 1-week window
- **Simulation flag critique is incomplete**: Acknowledges Natanz cover story implausibility but doesn't revise the 85% execution probability downward

**Verdict**: Best overall integration of data and simulation. The 72h probability (28%) aligns with historical base rates and fresh evidence. Minor deduction for 24h overconfidence.

## Response B (Pessimistic Lens) - GRADE: B+

**Strengths:**

- **Sharp scenario construction**: Natanz sabotage → Iranian retaliation chain is detailed (IRGC-IO frames as violation → Khamenei authorizes 40-60 missiles within 18h)

**Verdict**: Excellent on identifying failure modes, but too pessimistic on 24h window. The 72h assessment (12%) is defensible but lower than evidence supports.

## KEY INSIGHTS ACROSS RESPONSES

**Consensus findings:**
1. **72h is critical inflection point**: All responses agree ceasefire survival probability drops sharply after 72h (range: 12-58%, median: 28%)
2. **Lebanon scope ambiguity is structural fault line**: All identify this as primary collapse mechanism
3. **Hezbollah is most likely trigger**: 5/6 responses name Hezbollah as most likely breaker in 24-72h window
4. **Hormuz is sole de-escalatory action**: All note Iran keeping Strait open (10-15 vessels/day) as only genuine restraint signal

**Divergent assessments:**
1. **Natanz sabotage probability**: Range 40% (Optimistic) to 85% (Simulation/Pessimistic)
2. **24h survival probability**: Range 12% (Blindsides) to 72% (Neutral/Probabilistic/Optimistic)
3. **Regime collapse probability**: Range 3% (Historical) to 15% (Blindsides) in 1-month window
4. **Normalization track probability**: Range 5% (Blindsides) to 28% (Optimistic)

**Best single insight:** Response F's identification that "Mossad sabotage during negotiations is doctrine, not wild card" (backed by Stuxnet 2010, Natanz fire 2020, Karaj 2021). This reframes simulation's Natanz operation from speculative to expected.

**Most important fresh data point:** Response D's emphasis on "Russia coordinating with IDF on evacuating workers from Iranian nuclear facility"—this is the strongest indicator of imminent Israeli nuclear strike.
